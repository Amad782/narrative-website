Your VR Experience Made Easy

Trying something new can be a little intimidating, but dont worry, this page guides you through a simple step by step process to get your VR headset runnning.

Your first

steps

in VR

Unboxing

when unboxing your VR headset, the first thing to do is find an area to carefully unbox it. During the unboxing, each component will be in separate areas of the box, from the controller and the VR headset to the cables. Ensure to have dry hands and face as these are electronic devices and also handle them with care, since they are very lightweight and easy to damage. Keeping the original box can be beneficial as it can serve as a storing place for your headset and controllers.

Choosing the right space

before you put on your VR headset, you must find an appropriate space to use this headset so you dont injure yourself from your surrounding when immersed into virtual reality. We suggest that seniors should be seated when first putting on the headset, as there is a chance you may experience motion sickness. Whether you are sitting or standing up using the headset, the chosen space should have a good amount of room with no objects obstructing your environment. This would enable you to have an immersive experience with a sense of security.

Set up guide

Once you have picked an appropriate space, you can get right into setting up your VR headset. Simply locate the power button and press it to turn it on, there will be no need to charge it straight away as it will come charged out the box. Once its powered on you can put the headset on and adjust the straps of the headset to your preference and comfort. Next, you have to pick up the controllers, preferably somewhere relatively close to you to make things easier. Then you can follow all the instructions in the tutorial and set up process that is displayed to you.

Customising setting for your needs

You can make your experience suited to your needs by going into the headsets settings. The settings that can be adjusted are display settings such as increasing and decreasing the brightness, adjusting size of texts, audio and even live captions. There are many settings to play and test around with, so taking your time and finding the best settings for you will make your experience even better.
